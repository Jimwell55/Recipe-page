What is Lasagna?

Lasagna is a traditional Italian dish made with layers of pasta, rich meat sauce, creamy ricotta cheese, and melted mozzarella. It is believed to have originated in Italy during the Middle Ages, and today, it is one of the most beloved comfort foods around the world.

Variations of lasagna exist in different cultures, including vegetarian versions and seafood-infused recipes. The combination of flavors and textures makes it a favorite for family dinners and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (24 oz) jar of marinara sauce
  • 1 (15 oz) container of ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. Brown the ground beef in a skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened. Season with salt, pepper, basil, and oregano.
  4. Combine the ricotta cheese and egg in a bowl. Stir in half of the mozzarella cheese and Parmesan cheese.
  5. Assemble the lasagna: Spread a layer of marinara sauce at the bottom of a baking dish. Add a layer of noodles, followed by a layer of the ricotta mixture, meat mixture, and more marinara sauce. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used.
  6. Top with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  7. Bake for 45 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  8. Let cool for 10 minutes, then slice and serve. Enjoy!
